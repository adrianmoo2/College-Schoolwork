Drawing II: Refining Technique

Home /  Courses And Programs / Drawing II: Refining Technique
    • SHARE:
    • Twitter
    • Facebook
    • Google Plus
    • LinkedIn
    • Email a Friend

Build confidence and expand your drawing skills! Through use of different materials and techniques, students become comfortable with drawing as process. Topics include: proportion, foreshortening, perspective, nature of light, chiaroscuro and exploration of new forms of expression. Using wet and dry media, you will work on still lifes, self-portraits and other imagery, while strengthening your own voice. Experience the act of drawing as a way to reason through the creative process, learning how the simplest graphic marks can express complex concepts.

Note: Prerequisite: "Drawing: Focus on Perception" (ART 40166).
Bring graphite or charcoal pencils, drawing board w/ clips, 400 Strathmore white drawing pad 18"x24". Full materials list available at first class meeting.

Course Number: ART-40253
Credit: 3.00 unit(s)
Related Certificate Programs: Art and the Creative Process

There are no sections of this course currently scheduled. Please contact the Arts, Humanities, Languages & Digital Arts department at 858-534-5760 or ahl@ucsd.edu for information about when this course will be offered again.
